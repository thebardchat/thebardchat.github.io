// About the Book
22 noir-style vignettes. A detective who discovers he's the case. An old man who stands too close at the gas station. A garbage man who never looks up. A dream where a billion things almost make a shape. A doctor who doesn't diagnose. A missing scene that was never lost.
Dark but light. Cynical but comical. Written in third person by a dispatcher, father of five, and AI architect from Hazel Green, Alabama. The kind of book where you recognize everyone — especially yourself.
"You probably think this book is about you. You're right. It always was. It was never only about you."

// The Process
Shane talks. Claude shapes. Every scene started as a raw voice dump — recorded while driving, walking, living. Messy fragments. Tangents. The AI found the story inside the noise. It never invented a single thing.
The entire pipeline runs on a Raspberry Pi 5 in a closet in Hazel Green, Alabama. No cloud. No subscription. No writing retreat. Just a dump truck dispatcher who clocks in at 5am and writes noir fiction by night.
